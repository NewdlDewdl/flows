She pulled you toward her, her town, her car, her hands. The whole night, your one assignment is to match her openness and initiate back, so it is not all her.

Make your own moves

You already read her well. You just do not act on it. A move is closing the gap between reading and acting. Not a trick. You, finally acting.

  • From real wanting. Not because it is your turn or because she wants you. A forced move reads worse than none.
  • An offer, not a grab. Slow and visible so she can meet it or let it pass. Telegraphing is the consent.
  • Her response sets the pace. Met, keep going. Pause or pull-back, ease off. No sulking, no games.
1
Eyes.

Hold her gaze past comfortable, let her look away first. When she is inches from your face, do not bail by looking down.

2
Touch back.

Stop only receiving it. Brush her hand, then take it. Tuck her hair. A hand on her arm or knee.

3
One true thing, out loud.

Your hard one. One sentence: "I like being in your world." The in-person rep you keep dodging.

4
The kiss, if it is there.

Read her: lingering, soft, glancing at your mouth, not backing up. Close the distance slowly, or just ask, "can I kiss you?" Asking is not unsexy. Do not lunge.

When she leans back to check her work, that pause is the window. You do not have to take it. But notice it instead of letting it pass like the hug.

I'm not going to say the word back just to be even. But I'm here, and I want to find out where this goes. Together.
