Dextrose Protocol

pure glucose, zero bloat · cardio → lift · K-maxxing, salt OFF

Why salt is OFF on a K-maxxing day

The pinch of salt does two real jobs: SGLT1 co-absorption (a hydration assist) and replacing sweat sodium. But it is not free: 1/8 tsp is ~295 mg sodium, 15 to 20% of a 1,500 to 2,000 mg daily ceiling, and a short indoor session often will not sweat it back out. The real anti-bloat lever is the 6% or lower concentration, independent of the salt. Salt OFF by default; add it back only if you sweat through your shirt.

The three bottle recipes

PRE — 15 g dextrose (~1 heaped tbsp) in 380 mL (~13 oz) water. Thin, barely sweet.
INTRA — 25 g dextrose (~2 tbsp) in 420 mL (~14 oz) water. No salt on a K-maxxing day.
POST — 25 g dextrose + 30 to 40 g whey in ~420 mL water. Shake hard; drink within an hour.

The K-maxxing order

Order this, maximize K:Na

Potassium gluconate powder — 175 mg K per 1/2 tsp, 0 mg Na, 0 sugar. The clean lever; gentlest salt form, not the citrate that caused vomiting.

Organic coconut water powder — 600 mg K / 5 mg Na per 12 g (120:1, verified on the label). Food-matrix flavor at ~8 g sugar / 30 kcal per scoop. Brand-specific: other powders run ~13:1.

How: diluted in 8+ oz water, with food, in divided doses, start small. Safety: skip isolated K with kidney issues or on an ACE inhibitor / ARB / K-sparing diuretic.

Session safety
Hydrate through the session; this protocol is fuel, not a reason to push to failure. Keep cardio easy-to-moderate (LISS), avoid max-effort or heavy eccentrics on low sleep. No ibuprofen or other NSAID right after a hard session. Stop and seek care for dark or cola-colored urine, muscle pain out of proportion to the work, or real weakness.
