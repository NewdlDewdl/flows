Rung 7

The kiss, so you don't fumble it

Fumbling the moment is the only real failure here, and it comes from rushing or freezing. Neither happens if you slow down and read her first.

Read it (these mean she's there):

  • She holds eye contact and doesn't look away first.
  • She's slow to let go of your hand, or finds reasons to touch you.
  • She turns her body toward you, leans in, stays in your space.
  • The talking slows and the silence feels easy, not awkward.
  • Her eyes flick to your mouth. That one is the tell.

Then go, slow:

  • Stop talking. Hold the look a beat longer than normal.
  • Either say it, low: "can i kiss you?" or "i really want to kiss you right now." Asking is not weak; it's confident and it makes her feel safe, which makes the yes easy.
  • Or telegraph it: brush her hair back, move in halfway, and let her close the last bit. Letting her cover the final inch means she chose it.
  • Start soft and brief. Pull back a touch, read her, smile. If she comes back in, you're good. Don't narrate it, don't apologize, don't over-talk after.

No clear green light? Don't force a kiss. The hand, the jasmine in her hair, "i missed you," one cup two spoons, those already made tonight land. There is always the car.

Read her

Green, yellow, red

This is the actual odds-maximizer: escalate on green, ease off on yellow, stop warm on red. Wanted moves succeed; pushed ones end things. Reading her IS the skill.

Green
Leaning in, touching you back, sustained eye contact, mirroring you, body turned to you, slow to release your hand, laughing easy. Go to the next rung.
Yellow
Pulling back a little, shorter replies, glancing at her phone or the time, arms crossing, going quiet in a flat way. Don't push. Drop back a rung, re-warm, give her space. Often just tired from the flight, not a no.
Red
A "no," stiffening, turning away, moving your hand off. Stop instantly, zero sulk, stay warm like nothing's wrong. That composure is attractive and it protects every future night.
