Your pitch

Lead with this: you are a classical pianist who builds AI agents. That is what they remember.

One-liner

I'm Rohin, CS at UT Dallas and a classical pianist, so I'm either writing code or playing Rachmaninoff. I build AI agents and automation tools.

The 30-second

Hey, I'm Rohin, Fusion intern, studying CS at UT Dallas. Easy way to remember me: I'm a classical pianist and a compulsive builder, so most nights I'm either coding an automation tool or practicing Rachmaninoff, usually both. What I make is mostly AI agents and little command-line tools that kill an annoying problem, plus an open-source Mac app and a pile of hackathon projects. This summer I want to get my hands on agentic AI at real scale. What are you hoping to work on?

"What do you do for fun?"

Classical pianist, pretty serious about it. Rachmaninoff, Liszt, that world, and I run a small YouTube channel. It's the opposite of code, keeps me sane.

Then ask them

  • What track or project are you hoping to get on?
  • Where are you flying in from, what school?
  • First internship, or have you done this before?

Rules (read before you walk up)

  1. ~25 seconds, then stop and ask them something. The pitch starts a conversation, it doesn't finish one.
  2. One project if they want more. Don't list everything. Let them pull it out of you.
  3. Lead with the piano. It makes you a person, not a resume.
  4. Catch their name, use it once. Match their energy and length.
If you blank: name, school, "pianist who builds AI agents," one project, "what about you?" That's the whole skeleton.
