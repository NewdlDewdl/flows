StairMaster session

4am · wired · big day in ~5 hrs. ~45 min cap, then home and sleep.

StairMaster 30 min Light pump Hydrate hard No max-effort

Before you start

~1 pint water + a coconut water down now, bottle filled on the machine, sip throughout. Biggest safety lever today, you're dehydrated.

The session

StairMaster · 30 min
0-4Warm up, level 5-6. Stand tall, full foot each step, fingertips on the rail for balance only, don't lean on it.
4-26Steady state, level 7-9. HR ~130-145, or talk-test: short sentences, not gasping. Smooth cadence. Still wired? Add minutes, not level.
26-30Cool down, level 4-5, HR back under ~120.
Optional pump · ~10 min · only if you want it
  • Light + high-rep, leave 3-4 in the tank, controlled (no slow grinding reps).
  • Chest press machine — 3 × 15
  • Lat pulldown or seated row — 3 × 15, squeeze the back
  • Cable lateral raises — 2 × 18-20 (delts, zero risk, great pump)

Hard skip tonight

  • No max-effort / to-failure, no heavy slow negatives, nothing new-and-heavy. That's the one real risk.
  • No sprint intervals on the stairs. Spikes cortisol = more water = puffier, not leaner.
  • No added leg work. The stairs are your legs for today; long day on your feet ahead.
  • No pre-workout / energy drink / extra caffeine. Already wired. Water only.
  • No ibuprofen after. Rough on the kidneys in this exact stack.

Why light: meds + no sleep + a max-effort session is the bad stack you logged. Drop the max-effort and you defuse almost all of it.

Stop & get checked (next 1-2 days)

  • Dark / cola-colored pee
  • Muscle pain way past what the work should cause
  • Real weakness

Any of these = a CK blood test / urgent care, not tough-it-out. Unlikely if you keep it light and hydrate, but know them.

Out by ~45 min. The bloat deflates with sleep + tomorrow's potassium flood, not with punishment. Drain the tank, go home, sleep. She likes you, not your CK levels.
