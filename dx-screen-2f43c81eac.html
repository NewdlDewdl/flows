Why salt is off today

The pinch of salt does two real jobs: it co-absorbs glucose and water (a hydration assist) and replaces sweat sodium. But it is not free. That 1/8 tsp is ~295 mg sodium, 15 to 20% of a 1,500 to 2,000 mg daily ceiling, and a short indoor session rarely sweats it back out. The true anti-bloat lever is the 6%-or-lower concentration, independent of the salt. Keep salt off by default; add it back only when you sweat through your shirt.

The K-maxxing order

The clean lever and gentlest salt form, not the citrate that caused vomiting. e.g. NOW Foods Pure Powder.
Organic coconut water powder
600 mg K · 5 mg Na / 12 g = 120:1
Verified on the label. Food-matrix flavor at ~8 g sugar / 30 kcal. Brand-specific (Nutricost); others run ~13:1.

How: diluted in 8+ oz water, with food, in divided doses, start small. Safety: skip isolated K with kidney issues or on an ACE inhibitor, ARB, or K-sparing diuretic.

session safety
Hydrate through the session; this is fuel, not a reason to push to failure. Keep cardio easy-to-moderate, avoid max-effort or heavy eccentrics on low sleep, and skip NSAIDs right after a hard session. Stop and seek care for dark or cola-colored urine, muscle pain out of proportion to the work, or real weakness.
